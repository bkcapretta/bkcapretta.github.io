Film

During the spring of 2015 as a senior, my high school gave us six weeks to do whatever we wanted, so I decided to take on a film internship in San Francisco at Friday's Films.

In the six weeks there, I learned the film-making process from the initial creative development stage to the post-production stage. I worked hands-on with different cameras and lighting equipment. I also learned how to use editing software programs like Final Cut Pro. With the footage I filmed with the other two interns, I produced a few videos by the end of the internship.

  • The first video I created was intended to remind people in the office how to properly use the kitchen. Snack Etiquette.
  • The second video I filmed and edited focused on cleaning up in the office. Work Etiquette.
  • Finally, here is a video I edited of me rambling on about how much I love music and everything I've done with it. Bianca at Friday's.

Warm 1 Body

For four years in high school, I was the president of a project called Warm 1 Body. A certified 501(c)(3) non-pofit organization founded in 2010, Warm 1 Body provided caps, gloves, and socks annually for the homeless in Richmond, Oakland, Berkeley, San Francisco, and Marin County during the winter season. Each year I wrote a letter to family and friends to raise money, and since its first year in 2010, Warm 1 Body raised over $23,000 to warm around 2,100 people at $8 per set. After gathering all the materials, I contacted shelters to hand out the clothing myself.

The feet, hands, and head are the most vital body parts to keep warm, so while I passed out what felt like a small bag with caps, gloves, and socks, this gift truly made a difference in peoples' daily lives. Working on Warm 1 Body was most exciting for me because of the direct and real impact that I experienced. Nothing beats someone beaming a huge smile your way and knowing you made at least a little difference.
