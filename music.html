Shir Appeal

I joined Shir Appeal (Tufts' Only Coed Jewish A Cappella group - believe it or not!) my first year at Tufts and led as Music Director last year. This job included leading every rehearsal at seven hours per week, running the audition/callback process both semesters, arranging music for the group, and finally producing our most recent album, "Perspective"!

All of the music we sing has some kind of Jewish significance; we sing in many languages including English, Ladino, Yemenite Arabic, Portuguese, Russian, and Yiddish. I love spending time every week making music with this lovely group of people. As a music nerd, I must say that making harmony is simply one of the more beautiful things in life.

Check out the song I arranged and solo-ed in this past semester: "Like the Dawn". Last year I arranged a song in Ladino called "Yo M'enamori" originally performed by Casata Deleon. And I also got to sing a fun duet about healing with my friend Sarah in "Ana El Na".

Piano

I have been classically trained on the piano since I was five years-old. Some of my favorite pieces to play include:

  • Arabesque No. 1 by Debussy
  • Beethoven's Piano Sonata No.12 in A-flat major
  • Milonga del Angel by Astor Piazzolla
  • Six Morceaux Op. 11 - Waltz by Rachmaninoff
  • Arabesque by Schumann(and really anything by Schumann)
  • Rondo alla Turca by Mozart

More recently, I switched over to jazz piano and have picked up a bunch of beautiful songs:

  • Smile
  • Sometime Ago
  • Misty
  • I Got It Bad
  • How High the Moon
